Vigoli's Restaurant

History

While in college, Brooklyn, NY native, Liam Vigoli started his appetite for making people happy through food. He sold hotdogs from a wagon, now our “Salad Wagon” on the side of 23rd st. in Manhattan, NYC. Eventually Vigoli’s was established in 1954. Using his Sicilian family recipes and his charming personality, he made his mark in New york. In 1958, Liam took a break from the restaurant business to pursue another business venture, Act One Limousine. Liam decided to hang onto the décor and sentimental gifts that adorned the restaurant walls. Good thing he did, because in 1965, his daughter, Alissa Cerami convinced Fred to reopen Cerami’s as business partners and bring back the nostalgic energy and look of the original Cerami’s with a norhtern flair. Literally growing up in the restaurant business, Alissa acquired a passion for cooking and entrepreneurship. With the desire of making people happy with good food and welcoming staff, Liam & Alissa make a great team.
